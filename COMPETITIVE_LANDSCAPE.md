# Competitive Landscape: Org Chart & Headcount Planning Applications

> Research conducted April 2026. Sources include G2, Gartner Peer Insights, Capterra, Forbes Advisor, PeopleManagingPeople, and vendor websites.

---

## Table Stakes Features

These are the **minimum capabilities every org chart/headcount planning tool must have** — users expect them by default:

### Org Chart
- **Hierarchical tree visualization** with reporting lines (manager → direct reports)
- **Employee profile cards** showing name, title, department, photo, and contact info
- **Search & filter** across the org (by name, department, location, role)
- **Drag-and-drop editing** to move people/roles between teams
- **Data import** from CSV/Excel/spreadsheets
- **Export** to PDF, PNG, PowerPoint for sharing and printing
- **HRIS sync** — pull employee data from at least one major HRIS (BambooHR, Workday, ADP, etc.)
- **Role-based access control** — control who can view/edit what
- **Auto-layout** — chart reformats itself when people are added/removed/moved
- **Open positions** displayed on the chart alongside filled roles
- **Employee directory** as a companion to the org chart
- **Zoom/pan navigation** for large organizations
- **Mobile-responsive** or mobile app support

### Headcount Planning
- **Headcount tracking** — current vs. planned vs. actual headcount by department
- **Budget tracking** — total compensation cost per team/department
- **Requisition management** — track open roles through approval to fill
- **Basic forecasting** — project future headcount based on current hiring pipeline
- **HRIS + ATS integration** — pull current employee data and hiring pipeline status
- **Approval workflows** — route headcount requests through manager/finance/exec approval chains
- **Reporting & dashboards** — headcount by department, cost summaries, time-to-fill

## Modern UX Patterns

Best-in-class tools in 2026 follow these UX conventions:

### Navigation & Interaction
- **Infinite canvas with zoom/pan** (à la Figma/Miro) for large org exploration
- **Drag-and-drop reorg** — move people or entire sub-trees between managers
- **Click-to-expand** — collapse/expand subtrees to manage visual complexity
- **Side panel detail view** — click a person node to see full profile without leaving the chart
- **Keyboard shortcuts** for power users (search, navigate, expand/collapse)
- **Breadcrumb trail** showing navigation path through the hierarchy

### Visual Design
- **Photo-rich person cards** with avatar, name, title
- **Color-coded heatmaps** layered on the chart (tenure, comp band, performance, diversity)
- **Badge/tag system** for open roles, new hires, departing employees
- **Skeleton loading states** for large datasets
- **Dark mode** support

### Collaboration
- **Real-time multiplayer editing** (Creately, Miro-style)
- **Comments/annotations** on specific nodes or proposed changes
- **Share link** with view-only access for external stakeholders
- **Slack/Teams integration** for notifications on approvals, changes, comments
- **Audit trail / changelog** showing who changed what and when

### Data Entry
- **Inline editing** — click any field on a person card to edit directly
- **Bulk operations** — select multiple people/roles and batch-update
- **Smart suggestions** — auto-complete department, title, manager from existing data
- **Undo/redo** with full history

## Planning & Analytics

### Scenario Planning
- **Sandbox environments** — clone the current org state, make changes, compare to baseline
- **Multiple named scenarios** (e.g., "Aggressive Growth," "Flat Headcount," "10% RIF")
- **Delta analytics** — see exactly what changed between current state and scenario
- **Timeline-based scenarios** — plan changes across quarters, show projected org at future dates
- **Financial impact summary** per scenario (total comp, incremental cost, savings)

### Forecasting
- **Hiring velocity forecasting** — based on historical time-to-fill by role type
- **Attrition prediction** — ML-based models (Agentnoon) or rule-based (set expected turnover rate)
- **Budget burn modeling** — project when you'll exhaust headcount budget given current hiring pace
- **Seasonal planning** — account for cyclical hiring patterns

### Reporting & Dashboards
- **Headcount by department/function/location** — current, planned, delta
- **Cost analysis** — total comp, average comp by level, cost of open positions
- **Diversity analytics** — gender, ethnicity breakdowns overlaid on org chart and dashboards
- **Span of control reports** — manager:report ratios across the org
- **Time-to-fill** — by department, role type, recruiter
- **Pipeline health** — open reqs vs. candidates in pipeline vs. offers out
- **Customizable dashboards** — drag-and-drop widgets, saved views, scheduled email reports

## Collaboration & Workflow

### Approval Workflows
- **Multi-step approval chains** — e.g., Hiring Manager → VP → Finance → CHRO
- **Conditional routing** — different approval paths based on level, cost, department
- **Slack/email notifications** at each approval step (TeamOhana is best-in-class here)
- **Bulk approvals** for budget season
- **Audit trail** — full history of who approved/rejected and when

### Team Collaboration
- **Shared planning spaces** where HR, Finance, and hiring managers see same data
- **Role-based views** — Finance sees cost; HR sees people data; Managers see their team
- **Comments & discussions** on individual headcount requests or org changes
- **@mentions** to tag collaborators
- **Activity feed** showing recent changes across the org

### Change Management
- **Proposal/draft mode** — make changes in draft, get approval, then "publish" to live org
- **Change impact analysis** — before publishing, see who/what is affected
- **Communication tools** — generate announcements about org changes
- **Effective date scheduling** — schedule changes to take effect on a future date

## Integration Ecosystem

### Common Integration Categories

| Category | Key Systems | Why It Matters |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| **HRIS** | Workday, BambooHR, ADP, Gusto, Rippling, HiBob, Namely, SAP SuccessFactors, UKG | Source of truth for employee data; auto-sync org chart |
| **ATS** | Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Workable, iCIMS, SmartRecruiters | Track hiring pipeline against headcount plan |
| **FP&A / Finance** | Anaplan, Pigment, Planful, Adaptive Planning, NetSuite | Connect headcount costs to financial models |
| **Identity / SSO** | Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, OneLogin | Auto-provision org chart from directory; SSO login |
| **Communication** | Slack, Microsoft Teams | Notifications, approvals, bot commands for quick lookups |
| **Productivity** | Google Sheets, Excel, PowerPoint | Export/import data; embed charts in presentations |
| **Payroll** | ADP, Gusto, Paychex, Deel | Compensation data for budget modeling |
| **Performance** | Lattice, 15Five, Culture Amp, Peoplebox | Overlay performance data on org chart |

### Integration Depth Tiers
1. **Read-only sync** (most common) — pull employee roster from HRIS nightly
2. **Bi-directional sync** — push approved org changes back to HRIS (TeamOhana, ChartHop)
3. **Real-time webhooks** — instant updates when someone joins/leaves/moves in HRIS
4. **Deep data merge** — combine data from multiple systems (HRIS + ATS + Finance) into unified view

## Open Source Landscape

The open-source space for org charts is **weak on planning features** but has decent **visualization libraries**:

### Visualization Libraries (for developers building their own)
| Project | Tech | Stars | Strengths | Limitations |
|---------|------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **[bumbeishvili/org-chart](https://github.com/bumbeishvili/org-chart)** | D3.js, vanilla JS | ~1.2k | Highly customizable, framework-agnostic, supports Angular/React/Vue wrappers | Visualization only; no data management |
| **[dabeng/OrgChart](https://github.com/dabeng/OrgChart)** | jQuery | ~3.5k | Simple tree chart, drag-and-drop, export to PNG | jQuery dependency, dated API |
| **[dabeng/react-orgchart](https://github.com/dabeng/react-orgchart)** | React | ~900 | React-native implementation, customizable nodes | Basic functionality only |
| **[unicef/react-org-chart](https://github.com/unicef/react-org-chart)** | React, D3 | ~400 | Lazy loading for large orgs, export support | Archived/unmaintained |
| **[yWorks/react-yfiles-orgchart](https://github.com/yWorks/react-yfiles-orgchart)** | React, yFiles | ~100 | Professional quality, interactive, collapsible | yFiles requires commercial license for production |
| **JointJS / Rappid** | JS/SVG | — | Professional diagramming library, org chart demos available | Core is MIT, advanced features are commercial |

### Full Open-Source Applications
| Project | Description | Limitations |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **[Rolebase](https://rolebase.io)** | Open-source governance & org chart for self-managed teams (holacracy-inspired) | Niche use case (holacracy); limited traditional org chart features |
| **[Freeplane](https://docs.freeplane.org)** | Mind mapping tool that can create org structures | Not purpose-built for org charts; desktop-only |
| **Visual Understanding Environment (VUE)** | Academic tool from Tufts University for concept mapping | Java-based, dated UI, not HR-focused |
| **[freeorgchart.netlify.app](https://freeorgchart.netlify.app)** | Simple web-based org chart with Excel import | Very basic; no collaboration or planning features |

### Key OSS Gap
**There is no meaningful open-source alternative** that combines:
- Interactive org chart visualization
- Headcount planning & budgeting
- Scenario modeling
- Approval workflows
- HRIS/ATS integrations

This represents a **significant opportunity** for a well-built open-source or open-core product.

## Opportunity Areas

Based on the competitive landscape, here are whitespace opportunities for a standout product:

### 1. **Unified Org Chart + Headcount Planning (Open-Core)**
Most tools are either visualization-focused (Lucidchart, Organimi) or planning-focused (TeamOhana, Anaplan). Very few combine both well. ChartHop and Agentnoon come closest, but both are expensive ($8+/user/month and $199/seat/month respectively). An open-core product that nails both could capture significant market share.

### 2. **Developer-Friendly / API-First Architecture**
Current tools are designed for HR/Finance users with point-and-click UIs. There's room for a tool that's:
- **API-first** — every operation available via REST/GraphQL API
- **Embeddable** — org chart as a React/Vue component you can embed in your own apps
- **Extensible** — plugin system for custom data sources, visualizations, workflows
- **Self-hostable** — for companies with strict data sovereignty requirements

### 3. **AI-Native Planning**
TeamOhana has an AI copilot, but the space is early. Opportunities include:
- **Natural language org design**: "Show me what happens if we merge the Platform and Infrastructure teams"
- **Automated anomaly detection**: "Manager X has 15 direct reports — consider splitting"
- **Predictive attrition**: "Based on tenure and comp data, these 5 roles are at risk of turning over in Q3"
- **Smart role recommendations**: "Based on your growth plan, you'll need 2 more SREs by Q4"
- **AI-generated reorg proposals**: Given constraints (budget, headcount cap), suggest optimal team structures

### 4. **Real-Time Collaboration (Figma-Style)**
Most tools offer basic sharing but not true multiplayer editing. A product with:
- Cursor presence (see who else is viewing/editing)
- Real-time co-editing of org structures
- Threaded comments on specific nodes
- Proposal/review workflows (like GitHub PRs but for org changes)

### 5. **Lightweight & Fast Time-to-Value**
Enterprise tools like Nakisa, Anaplan, and Workday require months of implementation. An opportunity exists for a tool that:
- Gets you from CSV upload → interactive org chart in under 5 minutes
- Progressively adds complexity (connect HRIS later, add planning later)
- Self-serve onboarding without needing a sales call
- Free tier for small teams (<50 employees)

### 6. **Modern Tech Stack & Performance**
Many existing tools feel sluggish with large datasets. Building on a modern stack (React/Next.js, WebGL/Canvas for rendering, WebSocket for real-time) to handle:
- 10,000+ person org charts with smooth pan/zoom
- Sub-second search across the entire org
- Offline-capable with sync
- Beautiful, modern UI that feels like a consumer product

### 7. **Change Management & Communication**
Most tools stop at "plan the reorg." None help with:
- Generating communication plans for org changes
- Templated announcement messages
- Tracking employee sentiment during transitions
- Staged rollout of org changes (announce → effective date → follow-up)

### 8. **Integrated Compensation Intelligence**
Connecting org chart data with market compensation data (levels.fyi, Pave, Radford) to:
- Show how each role's comp compares to market
- Flag pay equity issues visually on the org chart
- Model the cost of bringing everyone to market rate
- Support compensation cycle planning directly in the org chart context

## Conclusion

The org chart and headcount planning space is **fragmented**: pure visualization tools don't plan, pure planning tools don't visualize, and the few tools that do both (ChartHop, Agentnoon) are expensive and enterprise-focused. There is a clear opportunity for a **modern, unified, affordable tool** that combines interactive org chart visualization with practical headcount planning, built on a modern tech stack with developer-friendly APIs and a self-serve onboarding experience.
